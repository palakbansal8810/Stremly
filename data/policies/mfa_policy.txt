Multi-Factor Authentication Policy

Section 1: Requirements
All user authentication systems must implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) using at least two of the following verification methods:
- Something you know (password)
- Something you have (mobile device, hardware token)
- Something you are (biometric verification)

Section 2: Implementation Standards
- Password complexity: minimum 8 characters with mixed case, numbers, and symbols
- SMS verification codes must expire within 10 minutes
- Hardware tokens must use TOTP or HOTP standards
- Biometric systems must have false acceptance rate < 0.001%

Section 3: Compliance Requirements
- All login flows must enforce MFA for admin accounts
- Regular user accounts may use MFA as optional but recommended
- Failed MFA attempts must be logged and monitored
